  • [Master Thesis] A Bayesian alternative to the FLiRTI model (2014)

    In the spring of 2014, I finished my Master's Degree in Biostatistics at the University of Montpellier. The last 4 months of the year were dedicated to the completion of a training course at the MISTEA laboratory under the direction of Christophe Abraham and Meili Baragatti. This training course was designed to understand the FLiRTI method and develop a Bayesian version of this method. FLiRTI aims to give an sparse estimate of the coefficient of the functional linear regression model. This coefficient being functional in nature, and the sparsity is understood differently from the case of multiple linear regression. The coefficient function will be considered sparse when it is null on some intervals and have a simple shape, for instance constant or piecewise linear. This study is an early work considering a Bayesian approach for the functional linear regression model when sparsity of the estimate is required.

  • [MSc Report] The influence of the G-quadruplex pattern in DNA replication (2013)

    In spring 2013, during the second half of my Master's Degree in Biostatistics, I did a training course supervised by Jean-Marc Lemaitre and Jean-Michel Marin and in collaboration with Romain Desprat and Vincent Coulon. This training course was designed to question a statistical link between the presence of a known sequence of DNA with its ability to replicate. This sequence (G-quadruplex) creates special connexions between elements of DNA, the double helix curling to bind onto itself. A hypothesis would be that the asperity and the tension induced by this formation could be linked to the location of the initiation of a new DNA replication.
