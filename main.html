I am an Associate Professor (Maître de Conférences) in Statistics at LMBP (Laboratoire de Mathématiques Blaise Pascal) at UCA (University of Clermont Auvergne). The main axes of my research are Bayesian statistics — initiating and developing tailored methodologies, and contributing to theoretical aspects — and applied statistics in agronomy, medical science, and cyber security. I am currently Head of the Data Science BUT (Bachelor of Technology) on the Aurillac campus.
Prior to my current position, I was a Research Associate in Statistics and Pathogen Phylodynamics at the Department of Mathematics at Imperial College London under the supervision of Oliver Ratmann. I work on HIV transmission and in particular I am focusing on determining the importance of risk factors in transmission networks and the proportion of HIV introductions into a population by demographic characteristics.
During my first non-tenured position, I did my PhD in the IMAG lab at the University of Montpellier under the supervision of Christophe Abraham, Pierre Pudlo and Meïli Baragatti. The title of my PhD thesis — Bayesian Functional Linear Regression — mainly deals with 1) how to sparsely estimate the coefficient function of the Functional Linear Regression model and in particular its support, 2) how to build an informative prior of this model from experts' knowledge and 3) establishing the consistency of this model.

    • Education

  • [PhD Thesis] Bayesian Linear Regression on Functional Data (2017). pdf abstract

    In spring 2014, I finished my Master's Degree in Biostatistics at the University of Montpellier. I then joined the MISTEA laboratory under Christophe Abraham and Meïli Baragatti to develop a Bayesian version of the FLiRTI method — giving sparse estimates of the coefficient of the functional linear regression model. The coefficient is considered sparse when it is null on some intervals and has a simple shape (constant or piecewise linear).

  • [Master Thesis] A Bayesian alternative to the FLiRTI model (2014).

    In spring 2013, during the second half of my Master's Degree in Biostatistics, I completed a training course supervised by Jean-Marc Lemaitre and Jean-Michel Marin, in collaboration with Romain Desprat and Vincent Coulon. The goal was to investigate a statistical link between the G-quadruplex DNA sequence pattern and DNA replication initiation sites.

  • [MSc Report] The influence of the G-quadruplex pattern in DNA replication (2013).

    Package R : STID

    Il s'agit d'un package ayant vocation à être utilisé par les étudiants du BUT STID lors de leur formation, ainsi que de les suivre dans leur utilisation future de R.

    Pour installer et utiliser ce package :

    • Cloner le git depuis l'URL https://forge.uca.fr/projects/stid_rpackage
    • Installer le package via le source

    Ce package est en cours de construction. Il se peut donc que 1) le source en .tar.gz ne soit pas disponible (il faut alors construire le package) et 2) que les fonctions du package n'en soient pas à leur état final.
